# Profit splits & scaling

:::warning
This is **v2**: the funded phase, not yet live. The closed beta has no real capital
and no profit splits. Definitions marked _open_ below are deliberately not invented;
they'll be set before the funded phase ships.
:::

## Profit splits by tier

On a funded account, profits are split between you and the firm. The split improves
with tier, and the higher tiers keep more for the trader:

| Tier | Eval fee | Account size | Profit target | Max drawdown | Profit split (you / firm) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Starter | $100 | $10,000 | 8% | 10% | 75 / 25 |
| Basic | $250 | $25,000 | 8% | 10% | 80 / 20 |
| Pro | $500 | $50,000 | 8% | 10% | 80 / 20 |
| Elite | $1,000 | $100,000 | 10% | 8% | 85 / 15 |
| Whale | $2,500 | $250,000 | 10% | 8% | 90 / 10 |

Your split is **locked to your account when it's created**, so later changes to the
firm's tier configuration never retroactively change an open account.

## The scaling ladder

Funded accounts grow as you stay profitable, reviewed on a monthly cycle:

- **A profitable month** → your account size grows **+25%**.
- **Three consecutive profitable months** → **+50%**.
- **Six consistent months** → your profit split improves by **+5 percentage points**,
  capped at **90 / 10**.

So a Pro trader who strings together a long, clean run can grow a $50,000 account
materially and improve their split over time, without paying for a higher tier.

:::info
What counts as a _"profitable month"_ and a _"consistent"_ run (the exact profit
threshold and any minimum-trading-days requirement) is an **open question** that will
be defined measurably before the funded phase launches. Pass-rate and scaling
economics are the firm's most carefully modeled risk, so these aren't numbers anyone
guesses at.
:::

## Why the splits look like this

The split is how the firm earns on the traders who succeed, alongside the
[evaluation fees](/evaluations/payment) from those who don't. Keeping 75–90% with the
trader is deliberately generous; the firm's edge is in the aggregate across many
accounts, not in squeezing any one trader. The
[economics](/how-it-works#why-the-model-works) only hold if good traders are paid
well enough to stay.
