# Glossary

The exact words Ultraprop uses, and what each one means. Where a term has a tempting
synonym that means something subtly different, that's noted too.

### Evaluation

The proving phase: a rule-bound test you trade through to earn a funded account. Not a
"challenge" or a "demo." See [The rules](/evaluations/rules).

### Evaluation fee

The tier-priced fee paid to start an evaluation. Not a subscription and not a deposit:
it's a one-time entry fee per attempt. See [Fees & payment](/evaluations/payment).

### Tier

Your evaluation's parameter set: account size, profit target, drawdown, daily loss
limit, leverage cap. Starter, Basic, and Pro today; Elite and Whale in the funded
phase. See [Tiers & accounts](/evaluations/tiers).

### Account size (notional)

The capital figure your account carries. It's *notional*: a standing the firm tracks
for you, never money you custody. In the closed beta no real capital backs it; in the
funded phase, real vault capital does, while your balance stays notional.

### Profit target

The gain you must reach to pass your tier (8% or 10%).

### Maximum drawdown

The most your account can fall from its starting balance before it's failed: a hard
rule that ends the evaluation when crossed.

### Daily loss limit

The most you can lose in a single UTC day (5%), reset at 00:00 UTC: a hard rule.

### Trade budget

The cap on the number of trades per evaluation (200). A limit, not a failure condition.

### Breach

Crossing a hard rule (drawdown or daily loss). A breach **freezes** the account
immediately and ends the evaluation. See [Passing & failing](/evaluations/outcomes).

### House tilt

The fixed +2 basis points applied against the trader on every fill: always disclosed,
identical for everyone, deliberately conservative. See
[The fill & slippage model](/transparency/fill-model).

### Bracket (TP / SL)

An optional take-profit and/or stop-loss attached to a position that closes it
automatically at your trigger. One-cancels-the-other. See
[Placing trades](/evaluations/trading).

### Genesis credential

Your single, non-transferable, on-chain record of what you've proven. It levels up in
place and accumulates history across attempts. Not a token, not an allocation. See
[The Genesis credential](/record/credential).

### Funded account

A post-pass account backed by the firm's real capital, with a profit split and payouts.
The funded phase is **v2** and not yet live. See [The funded phase](/funded/overview).

### Profit split

How profit on a funded account is divided between you and the firm (75/25 up to 90/10
by tier). See [Profit splits & scaling](/funded/splits).

### Payout

An on-chain profit distribution to your account that off-ramps to fiat automatically.
"Withdrawal" is fine as casual shorthand. Funded phase only. See [Payouts](/funded/payouts).

### Vault

The firm's real capital pool, funded by failed-evaluation fees, the firm's share of
profit splits, and yield on idle stablecoins. It's on-chain and auditable. Distinct
from the **treasury**, which collects evaluation fees.

### Oracle

An independent on-chain price source (Pyth) used to sanity-check market prices and to
gate trading when prices go stale. See [Prices & oracles](/transparency/prices).

### Inactive

An evaluation that auto-terminates after 7 days with no activity, recorded distinctly
from a pass or a fail.
