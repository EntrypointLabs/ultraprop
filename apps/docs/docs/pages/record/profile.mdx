# Your public profile

Every trader has a **public profile** — a single page that is your verifiable trading
résumé. It's the thing you share when you want to prove what you've done, and it
resolves from your account so anyone can open it without trusting you to vouch for
yourself.

## What's on it

- **The Genesis credential.** Your [credential](/record/credential) card — current
  level, tiers passed, and a link to verify the on-chain record directly.
- **Evaluation history.** Every attempt you've made, with its tier, outcome
  (pass / fail / inactive), date, and stats. Wins and losses both — the record is the
  whole record.
- **Trading stats.** Highest tier, total passes, simulated P&L, and when you were last
  active.

Because the page is built from on-chain state, it can't be edited into something
flattering. It says what happened.

## Your settings

On your own profile you'll also find your settings. The main one is **appearance** —
a System / Light / Dark theme toggle for the app. Light is a true institutional white,
dark is a low-glare cockpit; both are designed to keep the numbers legible and the
chrome quiet. Your choice persists across sessions.

## Sharing it

The profile is public by design — that's the point of a verifiable record. Share the
link with anyone who wants proof of your track record: a community, a desk, another
protocol. In the [funded phase](/funded/overview), the same profile carries your
real-capital history forward, so the record you build now compounds.

:::info
A profile is tied to a single trading account. Your [credential](/record/credential)
and history live with that account and accumulate across every evaluation attempt you
make on it.
:::
