# Passing & failing

Every evaluation ends in one of three outcomes. All three are decided by the
[contract](/transparency/enforcement), recorded on-chain, and reflected on your
[credential](/record/credential).

## Pass

You reach your tier's [profit target](/evaluations/rules) with every rule intact. The
moment your equity crosses the target with nothing breached, the evaluation is
complete.

On a pass you'll see a result screen confirming the outcome and your stats. Passing:

- **Levels up your [Genesis credential](/record/credential)** to the tier you just
  cleared.
- **Unlocks the next tier** in the ladder (Starter → Basic → Pro).
- **Moves you up the [leaderboard](/record/leaderboard).**
- In the funded phase (v2), opens a one-click path to claim a
  [funded account](/funded/overview).

## Fail (a breach)

You break a **hard rule** — your [maximum drawdown](/evaluations/rules) or your
**daily loss limit**. The evaluation ends immediately, the instant the breach is
detected. There are no warnings, no second chances, and no appeal — because the
outcome isn't a person's judgment, it's the rule's.

The fail screen names **exactly which rule was breached** and shows the trade that
triggered it, so the outcome is fully explicable from your own record. From there you
can **retry** — a retry is a fresh evaluation purchase: a brand-new account under the
same credential. You can re-enter at the same tier, drop to a lower one, or step up.

:::info
Your credential **accumulates history across attempts.** A failed attempt is part of
your record, not erased from it — every attempt's outcome is kept and feeds your
track record over time.
:::

## Inactive

There is no abandon button. If you stop trading, the evaluation simply waits. After
**7 days with no activity**, it auto-terminates with an `Inactive` outcome — recorded
separately from a pass or a fail. To trade again after that, you start a new
evaluation.

## What a breach looks like under the hood

Because Ultraprop fills against real venue prices, the two hard rules are checked
**after a fill settles** — the system observes the settled trade, recalculates your
equity, and if you've crossed the drawdown floor or the daily loss limit, the account
is **frozen** with the breach reason recorded. No further trade can be booked once an
account is frozen. This is covered in detail in
[On-chain rule enforcement](/transparency/enforcement).

## After a funded termination (v2)

In the funded phase, breaking a rule **terminates the funded account** — there is no
in-place revival. You re-enter the same way as any retry: by purchasing a new
evaluation under the same credential. See [The funded phase](/funded/overview).
