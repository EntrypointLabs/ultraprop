# Fees & payment

You pay once, up front, to start an evaluation. The fee is the firm's primary revenue
and the reason the [economics work](/how-it-works#why-the-model-works); it's what
funds the capital allocated to traders who pass.

## What you pay

The evaluation fee is set by [tier](/evaluations/tiers):

| Tier | Evaluation fee | Account size |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Starter | $100 | $10,000 |
| Basic | $250 | $25,000 |
| Pro | $500 | $50,000 |
| Elite (v2) | $1,000 | $100,000 |
| Whale (v2) | $2,500 | $250,000 |

The fee is charged once per evaluation attempt. A [retry](/evaluations/outcomes) after
a fail is a new attempt and a new fee.

:::info
**In the closed beta,** access is invite-led. **Starter** evaluations are opened with
an invite code; **Basic** and **Pro** are started by paying the evaluation fee. Elite
and Whale arrive with the [funded phase](/funded/overview).
:::

## How checkout works

Checkout is **fiat-first** and built so you never have to think about crypto:

1. You select a tier and see the **full rules contract** for it, profit target, max
   drawdown, daily loss limit, and (in v2) the profit split and refund terms, before
   you pay.
2. You pay **by card.** Behind the scenes, the payment is converted to USDC stablecoin
   and the evaluation fee is recorded on-chain to the firm's treasury.
3. Your evaluation account is created and you land in the cockpit.

Paying directly in USDC from your account is the underlying path, but card checkout is
the default and you never need to touch a wallet, a network, or a gas fee. See
[Verifiability](/transparency/verifiability) for what's happening underneath.

If a payment is rejected, you're returned to checkout cleanly: **no account is
created and you are never double-charged.** If a payment succeeds but provisioning
hiccups, the system retries safely without charging you twice.

## The refund-on-first-payout promise (v2)

In the [funded phase](/funded/payouts), there's a flywheel that traditional prop firms
made famous: **your first successful payout refunds your original evaluation fee.**

The logic is simple: fees from traders who don't pass fund the capital pool, and
traders who go on to earn a payout risk nothing net. This applies to funded payouts in
v2; it does not apply to the paper-trading closed beta, where there are no payouts.

## Currency

Fees settle in **USDC** (native Circle USDC). Card payments on-ramp to USDC
automatically. Whether SUI is accepted at a small discount is an open question for the
funded phase and not part of checkout today.
